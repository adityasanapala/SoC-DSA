1. How does recursion implicitly use a stack?
A. Everytime a recursion is called, the function goes up a level (like adding to a stack). While going back, it is like popping the top element.

2. Why is a queue required for BFS?
A. As BFS goes over the immediate neighbours, the elements have to get in line in terms of the distance from the vertex. So, a queue is used.

3. What are the key differences between DFS and BFS?
A. DFS visits the first unvisited neighbour of any vertex, making it go deep and then backtrack. On the other hand, BFS goes layer by layer. DFS uses a stack(recursion) and BFS uses a queue.

4. Why is a visited array/set necessary in graph traversal?
A. Since there is no hierarchy in a graph, it is imprortant to keep a visited array. Without it, traversing the graph is not possible as during the traversal, it might go back to vertex which has already been visited and over-count it. This may also cause a cycle, making it impossible to traverse the entire graph.
